I need some help. The core issue is that my Clipper NMEA repeater seemingly is not happy with the RMB data generated by opencpn. The overall connection is fine, the position works. But not SOG, XTE, etc.

Looking at the RMB messages opencpn generates they look like this:

$ECRMB, A, 0.000, L, Begin, 005, 6527.552, N, 02235.795, E, 0.373, 271.336, 0.000, V, A*04
$ECRMB, A, 0.000, L, Begin, 005, 6527.552, N, 02235.795, E, 12.926, 118.465, -0.000, V, A*1D<0D><0A>

My question is about the last part of that message: 0.000, V, A*04 . Here 0.000 is the speed to waypoint, 0 since I'm currently in harbour. After this there is V, A and a checksum.

However, when I look at the specs such as they are I only find one part, the arrival flag . It should at least be 'A' if n the arrival circle is entered. Some sources says it should be 'V' if not.

But opencpn sends two fields: 'A', 'V'. Something is broken here: me, specs or opencpn.

Any hint out there?

Alec Leamas (Jun 30 2025 at 10:37):

ok, found it: It's the NMEA 2.3 FAA Mode inidicator. Ergo: my specs was wrong

Håkan Svensson (Jun 30 2025 at 10:48):

Yes, OCPN understands both with and without the FAA mode while receiving RMB but transmit according to NMEA v. >= 2.3 i.e including FFA mode.

Håkan Svensson (Jun 30 2025 at 11:03):

It seems like it's an instrument to show data when navigation to a WP?
So you may need to follow an active track and filter the NMEA output port to the instrument to only transmit "EC". That will include OCPN's own RMC and RMB. And not the one created by a GNSS receiver as when you output "all".
