Manfred Radmacher (Jun 01 2024 at 15:19):

One example of these inconsistencies: when looking at the dialog box Options -> Ships -> Routes/Point , you will see that the word "waypoint" refers to two different navobjects:

Bildschirmfoto-2024-06-01-um-15.14.19.png

The instances circled in green refer to points, which are part of a route.
The instances circled in orange refer to objects, which have been created by "Drop Mark"

To me, it makes a lot of sense to call the one waypoint, since in a route "I am on my way to this point", and the other mark, since it marks a point of interest in the chart.

I am aware that the two objects have different names in navobj, however here I am just asking as a user to have a nomenclature, where different things are called differently.

I believe that waypoints and marks are nice and clear terms, which should then be used consistently throughout the entire user interface.

As a matter of fact, there are only a few instances, which ask for changes (in the GUI only I hope):

  • Route and Mark Manager -> WayPoint tab should be called Mark

Bildschirmfoto-2024-06-01-um-15.12.31.png

  • right click on a waypoint should open a menu with Mark in the title:

Bildschirmfoto-2024-06-01-um-15.13.27.png
Bildschirmfoto-2024-06-01-um-15.13.00.png

To corroborate this point, currently we have to write the following:
Drop mark creates a mark which is later on called waypoint. This shall not be confused with points in a route, which are also called waypoints. They look similar, but behave differently, even though waypoints (aka marks) can be added to routes as well. For instance, when deleting a route all waypoints of this route will be deleted, except those waypoints, who are actually marks.

Manfred
