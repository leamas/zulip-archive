Fabio Paoli (May 19 2024 at 23:47):

One comment on the "Table of starter Chart Sources": I see @Rick Gleason you've added four rows with all the possibilities for USA. A few other countries have two rows, one for ENC and one for OSM Raster. You've given options.

Although I see the value of letting new users know there are options and giving them the choice, I see more value in a Quick Start manual of only giving one option that gets a new user started and further down putting a link to the complete list of options. Or having the complete table further down the page.

For USA I would leave only "USA - NOAA & Inland charts" and "ENC". For the other countries I'd leave ENC only.

Another way of doing this without removing rows from the table would be to add a "Suggested for New Users" column, and marking only one of the rows, in my example "USA - NOAA & Inland charts"/"ENC" for USA.

My reasoning is that a new user might not know much about chart types, and might feel confused :confused: . I certainly did when I started with OpenCPN, I remember thinking "what the hell are all these ENC, RNC, etc?" :face_with_spiral_eyes: I had to Google before going back to the manual and continuing my chart install.

Thoughts folks?
