Pavel Kalian (Nov 20 2024 at 10:44):

First: OpenCPN is not a library, as in what I understand under that term, something you use to develop your own software.
OpenCPN, a desktop application, of course can open vector charts if they are in a supported format (S57, S63, CM93 or oeSENC)

Septian Wibisono (Nov 20 2024 at 11:06):

so, it is not possible to employ it's API to read S57 cell and dump it as bitmap?

Pavel Kalian (Nov 20 2024 at 11:24):

There are no defined APIs for this and there is no library build target.

Laro88 (Nov 27 2024 at 13:17):

We also use 7cs in some of our products :-)
There is no easy "use OpenCPN" as a nav chart rendering engine although it would be cool. Besides this, OpenCPN has no AML (Additional Military Layers) and the rendering engine is not certified for navigation (I probably could be - just need sponsors etc).
I use OpenCPN on my yacht and a bit in the office to validate / troubleshoot chart issues, seems wonderfully solid.
